Your Personalized Digital Well-being Action Plan

Building Sustainable Habits

Learn the science-backed principles for creating lasting change in your digital behaviors.

Start Small

Begin with tiny, manageable changes that feel almost effortless. Success with small habits builds confidence and momentum for bigger changes.

Example: Instead of "no phone for 2 hours," start with "phone in another room for 15 minutes while eating breakfast."

Stack Your Habits

Attach new digital well-being habits to existing routines. This leverages the power of established neural pathways.

Example: "After I brush my teeth (existing habit), I will put my phone in airplane mode (new habit)."

Make It Visible

Use environmental cues to remind yourself of your digital well-being intentions. What you see shapes what you do.

Example: Place a book where you usually put your phone, or use a sticky note reminder on your laptop.

Celebrate Small Wins

Acknowledge and celebrate your progress, no matter how small. This reinforces the positive neural pathways associated with your new habits.

Example: Give yourself a mental high-five when you successfully use the digital pause, or share your progress with a friend.

Plan for Setbacks

Expect occasional lapses and have a plan for getting back on track. Resilience, not perfection, is the goal.

Example: "If I spend too much time on social media today, I'll do a 5-minute mindfulness exercise and recommit to my boundaries tomorrow."

Find Your Tribe

Surround yourself with people who support your digital well-being goals. Social support significantly increases your chances of success.

Example: Join a digital wellness group, find an accountability partner, or share your goals with family and friends.

Monitoring and Adjusting Your Approach

Regular reflection and adjustment are key to maintaining long-term digital well-being. Use these tools to stay on track.

Weekly Check-in Questions

Progress Assessment:

  • What digital well-being practices did I successfully implement this week?
  • Which goals am I making progress on?
  • What challenges did I face, and how did I handle them?
  • What am I most proud of regarding my digital habits this week?

Adjustment Planning:

  • What's working well that I should continue?
  • What's not working that I should modify or stop?
  • What new strategy would I like to try next week?
  • How can I better support myself in achieving my goals?

Monthly Deep Dive

Goal Progress Review:

For each of your three main goals, assess:

  • How much progress have I made? (Rate 1-10)
  • What specific evidence shows this progress?
  • What obstacles have I encountered?
  • What adjustments do I need to make?

Habit Effectiveness Analysis:

  • Which habits have become automatic?
  • Which habits still require conscious effort?
  • Which habits should I modify or replace?
  • What new habits would support my goals?

Well-being Impact Assessment:

  • How has my overall well-being changed?
  • What improvements have I noticed in my daily life?
  • How do I feel about my relationship with technology?
  • What areas still need attention?
